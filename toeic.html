TOEIC

The TOEIC (Test of English for International Communication) is an english exam you will need to pass to graduate in Telecom Nancy. This exam tests your skills in different skills : listening and reading.

If you don't have TOEIC, you won't be able to graduate at TN, but don't worry, your english teachers won't forget to remind you to pass it !

Is it difficult ?

One thing is sure : you will need to work for it. During class, you will learn a lot of things about it, but if some notions remain unclear, this page is for you !

Below you will find a brief description of every notion you will need to know, and a link to a site that will explain it better than us !

  • Comparatives and superlatives : Comparatives and superlatives are often used in english, and it can be difficult to uderstand exactly how they work.
    Basically, for words with 2 syllables or less, you just add -er for at the end for comparative, and -est for superlatives, with a the before the word. (Fast --> Faster --> The fastest)
    When a word has more than 2 syllables, it is easier (<-- this is a nice comparative !) : Difficult --> More difficult --> the most difficult.
    The link : Comparatives and superlatives

  • Conditional : No matter when you pass the TOEIC, there will be questions about it. It can be separated in 3 categories. If you want to learn more, click on the link below.
    The link : Conditional

  • Gerund and Infinitive : It is difficult to explain clearly how it works, so we recommand you to see directly the link below.
    The link : Gerund and infinitive

  • Link words : They are important in english, and you need to know how to use them to express clearly your ideas. They are separated in several categories depending on what you want to express.
    The link : Link words

  • Nouns : Even if almost everybody know what a noun is, these can be tricky, especially when we begin to talk about countables and uncountables nouns.
    The link : Nouns

  • Prepositions : Some nouns, verbes or even adjectives need a preposition after them, that will change the meaning. To know them all is almost impossible, and learning them by heart won't be efficient. One of the best advice is to practice english.
    The link : Prepositions

  • Passive and active forms : Using the passive form instead of te active one can help you emphasize an element in your sentence. You need to know how to correctly use it.
    The link : Passive and active forms

  • Phrasal verbs : Just like prepositions, phrasal verbs are to be understood, because depending on the which you use, the meaning can change draastically.
    The link : Phrasal verbs

  • Present : It can appear simple at first sight, but the present presents (you get it?) some subtilities.
    The link : Present

  • Trap words and common expressions : These are particular cases you need to know.
    The link : Trap words and common expressions

  • Trap words and common expressions : These are particular cases you need to know.
    The link : Trap words and common expressions

  • Modal verbs : These verbs are used to express some ideas like capacity, ability, possibility...
    The link : Modal verbs

  • Question tags : Question tags are a specificity of english. They are used at the end of a question.
    The link : Question tags

  • Nature of words : Nature of a word is simply the fact of a word being a noun, an adjective, ...
    The link : Nature of words

  • Past : You will often need to express yourself in the past tense, so you need to know how it works.
    The link : Past

  • Pronouns and adjectives : If you don't manipulate them carefully, it can lead to several mistakes.
    The link : Pronous and adjectives

  • Future : Just like the past, you'll need to express your ideas with the future tense, so you better use it well.
    The link : Future
