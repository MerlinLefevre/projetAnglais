Embedded Software

Being able to develop software that are meant to be embedded is a sought skill. That's what Embedded Software (ES, or LE for Logiciel Embarqué in french) students do.

An embedded software is a complex software that needs to be reliable. It's main goals are to respond in real-time and to be interactive with the system it's embedded in, from programmable chips to a PC.

What can I do after I graduate with such a specialisation ?

A lot of companies will open their doors if you are specialized in ES. Here is a non-exhaustive list of fields you will have access to.

  • Research and Development
  • Aerospace and Defense
  • Communicating Objects Start-Ups

What will I learn in ES ?

Here is a list of the sujects you will learn in this specialisation

  • Automatic Control Engineering (Automatique) : Take last year exam, copy it, enjoy.
  • Network Physical Layer (Couche Physique des Réseaux) : Take last year exam, copy it, enjoy.
  • Models and Algorithms (Modèles et Algorithms) : Take last year exam, copy it, enjoy.
  • Smart objects (Objets Communicants Intelligents) : Take last year exam, copy it, enjoy.
  • Advanced networks and systems (Systèmes et Réseaux Avancés) : Take last year exam, copy it, enjoy.
  • Mobile Embedded Systems (Systèmes Embarqués Portables) : Take last year exam, copy it, enjoy.
  • Digital Audio Processing (Traitement Audio-Numérique) : Take last year exam, copy it, enjoy.

    • What do the students think of ES ?

    The ES specialisation is often the less wanted specialisation, for several reasons. First, it is low-level engineering, meaning that it is really close of physical platforms, so students either don't want to do ES, or don't know what is happening in there...

    Despite this reputation of a dark place where you are not sure what is going on, students who chose ES (they DO exist !) are really interested in what they learn.
